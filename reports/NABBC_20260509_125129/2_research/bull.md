## Let's Start With What the Bear Gets Right

Yes, the stock sold off hard. ~33% from 1600 to 1059. All moving averages are bearish. MACD is deeply negative. I'm not pretending those numbers don't exist — they do. **But here's what the bear misses: they're reading the *past* while ignoring what the charts are quietly whispering right now.**

Let me walk through the three signals that matter most:

### 1. RSI at 34.27 — The Bounce Is Being *Baked*

The bear says "RSI needs to drop below 30 for a confirmed oversold signal." Cool. But I'd argue we don't need to wait for the knife to hit the floor. An RSI that's been dragged from what was likely overbought territory down to mid-30s *after a 33% selloff* is already telling us that selling pressure is exhausting. The stock didn't fall gradually — it cratered. That kind of velocity burns out sellers fast. Every time RSI gets compressed this aggressively on a volatile name, the subsequent bounce has historically been sharp. We're not talking about a stock that went sideways into weakness. This was a *violent* flush.

### 2. MACD Histogram Narrowing — Momentum Is Dying

This is the one I want the bear to really sit with. Go back to the data: the MACD histogram went from **-33 in late April down to -8.72 now**. That's a narrowing of over 70%. A deeply negative MACD is scary, but a *diminishing* negative MACD is something entirely different. It means the sellers are losing steam on a *rate-of-change* basis. The bear can call MACD negative all day, but the trajectory of that negative number is the real story. When the histogram contracts while price is still falling, you're often looking at the final capitulation leg — the last gasp before buyers step in.

### 3. Price Hugging the Bollinger Lower Band

Price at 1059, lower band at 1034. That's a razor-thin cushion. The Bollinger Bands measure volatility-adjusted distance from a mean, and when price compresses against the lower band in a high-ATR environment, you get one of the most reliable mean-reversion setups in technical analysis. The bear's own report notes this: "near potential support." They literally flagged it — they just didn't connect the dots.
